User Need: Manage Risks / Stay Safe

103 guidance statements support this user need:

  • 2D alternative to 3D - As someone who cannot perceive content in three dimensions or who is sensitive to electronic 3D displays, I need to have three dimensional visual information presented using only two dimensions.
  • Adaptable captions - As a person who uses captions or subtitles to access spoken content, and who also has moderate visual impairment, I need to be able to customize the presentation of captions and subtitles so I can read them effectively.
  • Adjustable parts - As a user with limited reach, dexterity, or mobility, I need to be able to adjust the location and position of devices and controls or have all of them within easy view and reach.
  • Adjustable volume - As a user with limited hearing or in an environment that makes it difficult to hear, I need to be able to adjust the volume of the system to a suitable level.
  • Alerts in appropriate modality - As a person who is most sensitive to alerts in a specific modality, I need to receive alerts regarding the existence of critically important information in a specific modality (auditory, visual, or tactile) regardless of the modality of the information itself.
  • Appropriate brightness - As a user with limited vision, I need appropriate brightness for visually presented information for my viewing environment and situation.
  • Appropriate challenging - As a user who may have difficulty completing some tasks on my own, I need to have the complexity or difficulty set at a level which is challenging and motivating but not discouraging to me.
  • Appropriate speed - As a user with limited vision or difficulty tracking fast objects, I need to have dynamic images presented at a speed sufficient for me to perceive and track it.
  • Appropriate tactile settings - As someone who uses tactile output, I need to have control over tactile settings so that they better fit my needs (including adjustment of vibration amplitude, and choosing vibration patterns instead of frequency or strength attributes).
  • Audio characteristics - As a user with limited hearing, I need specific characteristics of audio content, cues, and feedback to fit my needs or to be adjustable.
  • Avoid auditory distractions - As a user with limited hearing or who is easily distracted auditorially, I need to avoid auditory distractions that may prevent me from focusing on a task.
  • Avoid cognitive overloads - As a user who may be easily overwhelmed by certain types of stimuli or situations, I need to avoid cognitive overloads.
  • Avoid contrast changes - As a user who has limited vision, I need to avoid when systems might change the foreground color to one that I cannot easily distinguish from the background color.
  • Avoid environmental glare - As a user with limited vision or who is sensitive to glare or extraneous lighting, I need to avoid glare from excessive brightness of the material or environment.
  • Avoid excessive heat - As a person who is using tactile features of a devices who may be sensitive to or overwhelmed by heat, I need devices to limit the amount of heat (or other radiation) felt at touch points so that it (a) does not interfere with my ability to perceive tactile information and (b) does not cause discomfort or injury.
  • Avoid excessive vibration - As a person who is using tactile features of a devices who may be sensitive to or overwhelmed by vibration, I need devices to limit the amount of vibration so that it does not interfere with my ability to perceive tactile information.
  • Avoid interruptions - As a person who may be distracted easily or who uses assistive technology that may seem unpredictable with certain interruptions, I need to be able to avoid unnecessasry interruptions, including browser pop-ups.
  • Avoid overwhelming audio - As a user with limited hearing or who is easily overwhelmed by certain types of audio stimuli, I need to avoid audio events that may overload my perception.
  • Avoid overwhelming haptics - As a user who is easily overwhelmed by certain types of touch or haptic stimuli, I need to avoid haptic effects events that may overload my perception or prevent me from completing a tactile task.
  • Avoid overwhelming visuals - As a user with limited vision or who is easily overwhelmed by certain types of visual stimuli, I need to avoid visual events that may overload my perception.
  • Avoid reflective glare - As a user with limited vision or who is sensitive to glare or extraneous lighting, I need to avoid glare from light sources in the environment reflecting off screens or other reading sources.
  • Avoid tactile distractions - As a user who may find some smells distracting, I need to avoid olfactory distractions that may prevent me from focusing on a task.
  • Avoid tactile distractions - As a user who may find touch and tactile sensations distracting, I need to avoid tactile distractions that may prevent me from focusing on a task.
  • Avoid visual distractions - As a user with limited vision or who is easily distracted visually, I need to avoid visual distractions that may prevent me from focusing on a task.
  • Background noise - As a user with limited vision, hearing or language skills, I need to be able to distinguish visual, auditory, or tactile information in the foreground from background noise (noise which may be of any modality).
  • Changing Content, State, and Value - As a user with limited or no vision, I need changes in content, state, or value to be programmatically indicated or I need a visual, auditory, and tactile indication.
  • Characters and Punctuation - As a user with limited language skills, executive fundtion or memory, I need the characters, symbols, and punctuation required to uderstand ambigous words and phrases.
  • Clean accessible name - Relates to interactive equivilent
  • Components findable - As a user with a disability, I need to be able to easily find components.
  • Contrast and scale - As a user with low vision, I need text and layout design to appear in a custom or standard high contrast mode and at higher scale.
  • Default fonts - As a user with limited vision or other difficulty reading, I need text to be written using a font and style that supports readaibility (example: avoids all caps, italics, and very light weights).
  • Distinguish auditory components - As a user who is using assistive technology or accessibility features, I need to be able to prevent interference from other devices.
  • Distinguish auditory components - As a user who is listening to multiple auditory components or channels (potentially including assistive technology or accessibility features), I need to be able to clearly perceive and distingish the various auditory components and not have them interfere with each other.
  • Distinguish if actionable - As a user with a disability, I need to be able to distinguish between actionable and non-actionable components, including enabled/disabled controls and different types of components that are/are not actionable.
  • Distinguish tactile components - As a user who is using multiple tactile components or channels, I need to be able to clearly perceive and distingish the various tactile components.
  • Distinguish visual components - As a user who is using multiple visual components or channels (potentially including assistive technology or accessibility features), I need to be able to clearly perceive and distingish the various visual components and not have them interfere with each other.
  • Emotional Coping - As a user who may have difficulty completing some tasks on my own, I need to to have assistance in coping with my emotional reactions to using the system.
  • Encouragement on task progress - As a user who may have difficulty completing some tasks on my own, I need to receive encouragement that the I am progressing with a task .
  • Error Identification - As a user with a sensory disability, I need errors to be identified sensorily and programmatically.
  • Focus visible - As a user with limited vision, I need a visible indicator of the location of the focus(es) with sufficient contrast.
  • Follow common interface behavior conventions - As a user with limited or no vision, or difficulty being distracted by and understanding novel layouts and interfaces, I need content and interfaces that are consistent and follow user interface interaction behavior and feedback conventions with which I am familiar.
  • Follow common layout conventions - As a user with limited or no vision, or difficulty learning or getting an overview of and understanding novel layouts and interfaces, I need content and interfaces that are consistent and follow layout conventions with which I am familiar.
  • Font - As a user with limited vision or other difficulty reading, I need text to be available in a font and style that matches my needs.
  • Goal & Value reminder - As a user who may have difficulty completing some tasks on my own, I need to receive periodic reminders of the goal being worked on and its value.
  • Information on task progress - As a user who may have difficulty completing some tasks on my own, I need to receive information regarding the progress of completing a task.
  • Keyboard commands displayed - As a person who uses a keyboard for interaction, I need to have available keyboard commands clearly displayed.
  • Language of Content - As a user with no vision or limited language skills, I need the language of content to be programmatically determinable.
  • Limited olfactory equivalents - As a user with limited sense of smell or gustation, I need olfactory and gustatory content presented programmatically or I need equivalent visual, auditory, and tactile versions.
  • Limited vision distinguish distractions - As a user with limited vision, I need content that is not related to my task or from a different source than the main content, to be clearly indicated both programmatically, visually with high luminocity, and with equivalent auditory and tactile versions.
  • Limited vision interactive equivalent - Overlap with programmatic structure and relationships.
  • Limited vision interactive equivalent - As a user with limited vision, I need interactive components to have meaningful and equivalent sensory and programmatic indicators of their purpose (label), role and value. I need changes in content, state, or value to be programmatically indicated and I need a visual, auditory, and tactile indication.
  • Limited vision locate focus - As a user with limited vision, As a user with limited vision, I need the current location of the focus(es) to be presented programmatically, visually with high contrast/luminosity, and not visually obscured by other content.
  • Magnification - As a user with limited vision, I need to have the magnification of content match my needs given my current viewing situation and environment.
  • Meaningful Sequence - As a user with no vision, limited vision, limited fine motor control or no motor control, I need content to be available in a meaningful sequence both sensorily and programmatically.
  • Meaningful organization - As a user with a cognitive and learning disability or an assistive technology user, I need my page and site contents and structure to be organized the site into logical, cohesive sections both sensorily and programmatically.
  • Monoaural information - As a user with limited hearing, I need to have multi-channel audio information, including directional information, available in a monoaural form.
  • Multi-frequency events - As a user with limited hearing, I audio alerts and other events to be multi-frequency, including frequencies where my hearing may be better.
  • Mute all audio - As a person using a system with audio output, I need the ability to silence or mute all audio output.
  • No hearing equivalents - As a user without hearing or with limited hearing, I need auditory content presented programmatically or I need equivalent visual and tactile versions.
  • No tone differentiation - As a user who cannot differentiate quickly between different tones (frequencies), I need alerts, notifications, and other audio events to have characteristics other than tone differentiation if I need to distinguish between different audio events or obtain information from such audio events.
  • No vision and no hearing equivalents - As a user with neither vision nor hearing, I need visual and auditory content presented programmatically or I need equivalent tactile versions.
  • No vision equivalents - As a user without vision, I need visual content presented programmatically or I need equivalent auditory and tactile versions.
  • No vision interactive equivalent - As a user with no vision, I need interactive components to have meaningful and equivalent sensory and programmatic indicators of their purpose (label), role and value. I need changes in content, state, or value to be programmatically indicated and I need an auditory and tactile indication.
  • No-color equivalents - As a user with low vision or color vision deficiency, I need information provided through chromaticity to be available in another visual manner.
  • Nonauditory Instruction References - As a user with a limited or no hearing, I need instructions that reference auditory information to include an additional programmatic reference or provide equivalent visual or tactile references
  • Nongustatory Alternate Intruction References - As a user with a limited sense of smell and gustation, I need instructions that reference smell or taste to include an additional programmatic reference or provide equivalent visual, auditory, and tactile references
  • Nontactile Alternate Intruction References - As a user with a limited tactile sensitivity, I need instructions that reference touch to include an additional programmatic reference or provide equivalent visual or auditory references
  • Nontext Contrast - As a user with low vision, I need user interface components and graphical objects to have sufficient contrast with adjacent colors.
  • Nonvisual Intruction References - As a user wtih limited or no vision, I need instructions that reference visual information to include an additional programmatic reference or provide equivalent auditory or tactile references.
  • Nonvisual and Auditory Alternate Intruction References - As a user with no vision and no hearing, I need instructions that reference auditory or visual information to include an additional programmatic reference or provide equivalent tactile references
  • Notifications in auditory alternatives - As a user with low or no hearing, I need notifications to work with visual or tactile equivalents for auditory content.
  • Output modality choice - As a user with limited or no vision or with limited or no hearing, I need to be able to choose modalities to be used for outputs from the system.
  • Perceive auditory content - As a user with low or no hearing, I need visual or tactile equivalents for auditory content.
  • Perceive focus - As a user who has a disability, I need to perceive the interface element that currently has focus.
  • Physical layout descriptions - As a user with no vision, I need to have information describing location and layout of operable parts.
  • Preparing for tasks - As a user with a cognitive and learning disabiltiy, I need to know what I will need to complete a task before I begin. .
  • Programmatic structure and relationships - As a user with no vision, limited vision, limited fine motor control or no motor control, I need structure and relationships (title, labels, section headings) presented sensorily and programmatically.
  • Purposeful labels - As a user with a disability, I need to have labels that make the function of each component be easily perceivable.
  • Recognizable signals - As a user with a disability, I need recognizable signals (including feedback and cues) for different alerts or other messages that use signals.
  • Select audio stream - As someone with no or limited vision or with limited hearing, I need to have the ability to select and deselect different audio streams.
  • Separate identification & activation - As someone who has a disability, I need to have separate means of identifying and then activating controls.
  • Separate selection & activation - As someone who has a disability, I need to have separate means of selecting and then activating controls.
  • Sequence of task steps - As a user who may have difficulty completing some tasks on my own, I need to receive prompts identifying the appropriate next step to perform for a task.
  • Sign language characteristics - As someone who understands sign language, I need sign language content in a sign language that I understand, with sufficient size, resolution, speed, and contrast.
  • Signposts - As a user with a disability, I need clear signposts to indicate specific locations where functionalities can be found.
  • Simple login - As a user with a cognitive and learning disabiltiy, I need a simple way to log in.
  • Status indicators - As a person with no or limited vision or with no or limited hearing, I need the status of toggle controls available to me in a modality (e.g., tactile, visual, auditory, etc.) available to me.
  • Successes Acknowledged - As a user who may have difficulty completing some tasks on my own, I need to have even small successes acknowledged to increase self-esteem.
  • Supplement auditory location cues - As a user with no hearing, I need auditory cues to location in space to be represented in other forms, such as visual, textual, or haptic cues.
  • Synchronized auditory alternatives - As a user with limited hearing or limited language skills, I need alternate content, such as subtitles, to be synchronized with the main content.
  • Tactile symbol clarity - As someone who feels tactile symbols, I need to have tactile symbols with an appropriate level of clarity so that I can easily perceive, differentiate, or read them.
  • Tactile text speed - As someone who reads tactile text, I need to have tactile text at an appropriate reading speed.
  • Text Contrast - As a user with low vision, I need text to have sufficient contrast with its background.
  • Text alignment - As a user with limited vision or other difficulty reading, I need text not to be set to full justify.
  • Text resizable - As a user with limited vision, I need to be able to resize text to fit my needs in my current viewing situation and environment.
  • Timeout Warning - As a user with no vision or limited vision, I need to be notified sensorily and programatically when a timeout is about to occur and has occurred.
  • Title - As a user with no vision or limited vision; limited attention and concentration, or limited executive functions, I need the page or screen to have a visual and programmatic title that describes the purpose of the page.
  • Unchanging button functionality - As a user with no vision, limited vision, or a cognitive disability, I need each button or key to have its own functionality rather than functionality that changes depending on context.
  • Unexepected movement - As a user with a cognitive and learning disability, I need content and controls to remain in the same relative location and not move unexpectedly.
  • Visual/tactile feedback at control location - As a user with a disability who might not be paying attention to other output devices, I need to have visual or tactile feeback occur at the same location as the control I am using.
  • Voice commands displayed - As a person who uses dictation and voice commands, I need to have available voice commands clearly displayed.
  • Within viewing range - As a user with short stature, mobility issues, or other reasons that I cannot position myself at an expected viewing angle, I need to have information and controls within my viewing range.
  • focus indicated - As a user with no vision, I need the current location of the focus(es) to be presented programmatically.
