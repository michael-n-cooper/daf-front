Functional Need: Limited vision

45 guidance statements support this functional need:

  • 2D alternative to 3D - As someone who cannot perceive content in three dimensions or who is sensitive to electronic 3D displays, I need to have three dimensional visual information presented using only two dimensions.
  • Adaptable captions - As a person who uses captions or subtitles to access spoken content, and who also has moderate visual impairment, I need to be able to customize the presentation of captions and subtitles so I can read them effectively.
  • Appropriate brightness - As a user with limited vision, I need appropriate brightness for visually presented information for my viewing environment and situation.
  • Appropriate speed - As a user with limited vision or difficulty tracking fast objects, I need to have dynamic images presented at a speed sufficient for me to perceive and track it.
  • Audio Control - As a user with no vision who uses a screen reader, I must be able to control auditory content presentation (volume, speed, stop/pause)
  • Avoid contrast changes - As a user who has limited vision, I need to avoid when systems might change the foreground color to one that I cannot easily distinguish from the background color.
  • Avoid environmental glare - As a user with limited vision or who is sensitive to glare or extraneous lighting, I need to avoid glare from excessive brightness of the material or environment.
  • Avoid interruptions - As a person who may be distracted easily or who uses assistive technology that may seem unpredictable with certain interruptions, I need to be able to avoid unnecessasry interruptions, including browser pop-ups.
  • Avoid overwhelming visuals - As a user with limited vision or who is easily overwhelmed by certain types of visual stimuli, I need to avoid visual events that may overload my perception.
  • Avoid reflective glare - As a user with limited vision or who is sensitive to glare or extraneous lighting, I need to avoid glare from light sources in the environment reflecting off screens or other reading sources.
  • Avoid visual distractions - As a user with limited vision or who is easily distracted visually, I need to avoid visual distractions that may prevent me from focusing on a task.
  • Background noise - As a user with limited vision, hearing or language skills, I need to be able to distinguish visual, auditory, or tactile information in the foreground from background noise (noise which may be of any modality).
  • Changing Content, State, and Value - As a user with limited or no vision, I need changes in content, state, or value to be programmatically indicated or I need a visual, auditory, and tactile indication.
  • Clean accessible name - Relates to interactive equivilent
  • Contrast and scale - As a user with low vision, I need text and layout design to appear in a custom or standard high contrast mode and at higher scale.
  • Default fonts - As a user with limited vision or other difficulty reading, I need text to be written using a font and style that supports readaibility (example: avoids all caps, italics, and very light weights).
  • Follow common interface behavior conventions - As a user with limited or no vision, or difficulty being distracted by and understanding novel layouts and interfaces, I need content and interfaces that are consistent and follow user interface interaction behavior and feedback conventions with which I am familiar.
  • Follow common layout conventions - As a user with limited or no vision, or difficulty learning or getting an overview of and understanding novel layouts and interfaces, I need content and interfaces that are consistent and follow layout conventions with which I am familiar.
  • Font - As a user with limited vision or other difficulty reading, I need text to be available in a font and style that matches my needs.
  • Hover Content - As a user with limited vision, I need control content that appears on hover or focus.
  • Input modality choice - As a user with a disability, I need to be able to choose modalities to be used for inputs to the system.
  • Large scale layout - As a user of large scale display modes, I visual relationship aspects of the layout to be preserved, and content not to be clipped.
  • Limited vision distinguish distractions - As a user with limited vision, I need content that is not related to my task or from a different source than the main content, to be clearly indicated both programmatically, visually with high luminocity, and with equivalent auditory and tactile versions.
  • Limited vision interactive equivalent - Overlap with programmatic structure and relationships.
  • Limited vision interactive equivalent - As a user with limited vision, I need interactive components to have meaningful and equivalent sensory and programmatic indicators of their purpose (label), role and value. I need changes in content, state, or value to be programmatically indicated and I need a visual, auditory, and tactile indication.
  • Limited vision locate focus - As a user with limited vision, As a user with limited vision, I need the current location of the focus(es) to be presented programmatically, visually with high contrast/luminosity, and not visually obscured by other content.
  • Magnification - As a user with limited vision, I need to have the magnification of content match my needs given my current viewing situation and environment.
  • Meaningful Focus Order - As a user with a mobility disability, I need interactive content to be available in a meaningful sequence that facilitates operation
  • Meaningful Sequence - As a user with no vision, limited vision, limited fine motor control or no motor control, I need content to be available in a meaningful sequence both sensorily and programmatically.
  • No reliance on specific sense to avoid injury - As a user with a sensory limitation, I need to to have systems that do not rely on a specific sense to avoid injury.
  • No vision and no hearing equivalents - As a user with neither vision nor hearing, I need visual and auditory content presented programmatically or I need equivalent tactile versions.
  • No-color equivalents - As a user with low vision or color vision deficiency, I need information provided through chromaticity to be available in another visual manner.
  • Nontext Contrast - As a user with low vision, I need user interface components and graphical objects to have sufficient contrast with adjacent colors.
  • Nonvisual Intruction References - As a user wtih limited or no vision, I need instructions that reference visual information to include an additional programmatic reference or provide equivalent auditory or tactile references.
  • Output modality choice - As a user with limited or no vision or with limited or no hearing, I need to be able to choose modalities to be used for outputs from the system.
  • Programmatic structure and relationships - As a user with no vision, limited vision, limited fine motor control or no motor control, I need structure and relationships (title, labels, section headings) presented sensorily and programmatically.
  • Select audio stream - As someone with no or limited vision or with limited hearing, I need to have the ability to select and deselect different audio streams.
  • Status indicators - As a person with no or limited vision or with no or limited hearing, I need the status of toggle controls available to me in a modality (e.g., tactile, visual, auditory, etc.) available to me.
  • Text Contrast - As a user with low vision, I need text to have sufficient contrast with its background.
  • Text alignment - As a user with limited vision or other difficulty reading, I need text not to be set to full justify.
  • Text resizable - As a user with limited vision, I need to be able to resize text to fit my needs in my current viewing situation and environment.
  • Title - As a user with no vision or limited vision; limited attention and concentration, or limited executive functions, I need the page or screen to have a visual and programmatic title that describes the purpose of the page.
  • Unchanging button functionality - As a user with no vision, limited vision, or a cognitive disability, I need each button or key to have its own functionality rather than functionality that changes depending on context.
  • Visual Presentation Control - As a user with limited vision, I must be able to control the visual presentation of content includiong color, orientation, viewport, formatting, spacing, and size.
  • scrolling - As a user with limited vision, I need scrolling limited to a single direction.
